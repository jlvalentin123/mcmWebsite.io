BACHELOR OF MULTIMEDIA ARTS

The Bachelor of Arts in Communication program adheres to excellence and relevance and aspires to transform students into proficient communicators who catalyze positive change in society through their understanding of the evolving media landscape. Students undergo a rigorous curriculum that allows them to take deep dives on the theory and practice of communication through diverse and emerging media platforms. The program exposes students to various disciplines including media production, broadcasting, journalism, advertising, and communication research taught by expert faculty members and media practitioners.

A wide array of elective courses, research exposure, and internship opportunities enable students to design their own learning experiences based on their personal interests and learning goals. BPROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

Three years after graduation, graduates of the BA Communication Program shall be:

1. Equipped with theoretical and technical competencies relevant in the practice of ethical and effective communication applicable in various communication or media professions and contexts that can support the industry, academe, government, or community;

2. Collaborators and innovators in the field, leading or participating in efforts to address social, technical, ethical and business challenges; and

3. Engaged in life-long learning and professional development.

STUDENT OUTCOMES

At the end of the program, graduates of the BA Communication Program will be able to:

1. Produce communication plans and materials in different formats for various platforms and purposes;

2. Conduct communication/media research and evaluation;

3. Adhere to ethical standards, practices, responsibilities and accountabilities of the communication profession;

4. apply communication theories/models, principles, practices, and tools in relevant and development work;

5. Collaborate effectively in multidisciplinary and multicultural teams;

6. Effectively communicate orally and in writing using both English and Filipino;

7. Preserve and promote Filipino historical and cultural heritage;

8. Recognize the need for and demonstrate the ability for lifelong learning and service orientation; and

9. Participate in the generation of new knowledge or in research and development projects and activities.
